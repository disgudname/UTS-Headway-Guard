How these numbers are calculated

Arrival detection

A bus is counted as arrived when it enters the stop's detection bubble (a small zone centered on the stop) and its speed drops below 0.5 m/s — meaning it's physically at the curb, doors open. TransLoc fires arrival earlier, while the bus is still on final approach.

Departure detection

A bus is counted as departed when it exits the detection bubble — i.e., the moment it pulls away from the curb. TransLoc fires departure once the bus is roughly 600–650 ft from the stop, which can lag by 1–3 minutes if traffic holds the bus after it leaves.

Loop times

Loop time = time from a vehicle's departure at this stop to its next arrival here (same vehicle, one full circuit). Because our departure fires at the curb and TransLoc's fires ~600 ft out, our loop times run ~1 minute longer on average — that extra minute is real road time between the curb and the 600 ft mark, not an error.

Wait times

Wait time = time between any two consecutive bus arrivals at this stop — what a passenger would experience if they just missed one. Computed the same way as loop times but across all vehicles.
